Things To Do

What the Bay does best

Hand-picked experiences for every type of traveler — outdoor adventurer, foodie, culture seeker, or day-tripper.

🌉
Landmarks
Walk the Golden Gate Bridge

The south end offers the best angle. Go on a clear morning before the fog rolls in — usually before 10am.

Free
🌲
Nature
Hike Marin Headlands

Miles of coastal trails with sweeping bay views, WWII bunkers, and a black-sand beach that's rarely crowded.

Free entry
🍷
Day Trip
Napa & Sonoma Wine Tours

World-class wineries less than 90 minutes from the city. Book tastings in advance, especially weekends.

$25–$85+
🥟
Food
Dim Sum in the Richmond

SF's Richmond District has some of the best dim sum outside Hong Kong. Arrive early or expect a long wait.

Under $20
⛵
Water
Bay Cruise & Alcatraz

The audio tour on Alcatraz is genuinely excellent. Book tickets at least 2 weeks ahead in peak season.

$45/person
🎨
Culture
SFMOMA & Mission Murals

World-class modern art inside, then walk the Mission for street murals that rival any gallery wall.

$25 SFMOMA
🌅
Views
Twin Peaks at Sunset

Best 360° panoramic view of the city. Dress warm — it's always windier and colder up top than you expect.

Free
🚴
Active
Bike to Sausalito

Rent bikes at Fisherman's Wharf, ride across the Golden Gate to Sausalito, then catch the ferry back.

~$35 rental

How to spend your time

Itinerary 01
Walk the Bridge & Back
⏱ 2–3 hours
9:00 AM
Golden Gate Bridge Welcome Center
Start at the south visitor plaza — grab a coffee and walk out onto the bridge.
9:30 AM
Walk the Bridge
It's 1.7 miles round-trip on foot. Stop midway for photos — the bay view is unreal.
10:30 AM
Fort Point
Walk down beneath the bridge to the Civil War-era fort. Free entry, incredible angles.
11:30 AM
Crissy Field
Walk the restored wetlands path with bridge views the whole way.
Local tip: Go early — fog usually burns off by 10am and parking fills up fast. Take the 28 bus from the city to avoid the hassle entirely.

The Full Headlands Day
⏱ Full day
8:00 AM
Battery Spencer

9:30 AM
Hawk Hill Trail

11:30 AM
Rodeo Beach
A wild black-and-green pebble beach at the end of the headlands. Almost no one makes it here.
1:00 PM
Lunch at Sausalito

3:00 PM
The Presidio
Cross back and explore Presidio trails, the Walt Disney Family Museum, or Lucasfilm HQ.
5:30 PM

Things to Know

🌤️
Weather
Notoriously foggy and windy even in summer. Bring a windproof jacket regardless of the forecast. Morning fog usually clears by 11am.
🅿️
Parking
Bridge parking fills by 9am on weekends. Marin Headlands lots are free but limited. Take the bus or bike if you can.
♿
Accessibility
The bridge walkway is accessible. Most headlands trails are unpaved and hilly — Crissy Field and the bridge plaza are wheelchair friendly.
💰
Budget Tips
Almost everything here is free — bridge walk, Presidio trails, headlands hikes, Fort Point. Bring your own food and water.
⚠️
Watch Out For
Strong winds on the bridge (hold your hat). Sneaker waves at Rodeo Beach — never turn your back on the ocean. Wildlife on trails.
📅
Best Season
September and October are the clearest months. Spring brings wildflowers on the headlands. Summer fog can be heavy but atmospheric.

How to Get Around

Bus
Muni 28 & 29
Bus 28 runs from downtown SF to the bridge south entrance. No car needed and often faster.
Shuttle
PresidiGo Shuttle
Free shuttle within the Presidio connecting trailheads, transit center, and Crissy Field. Runs daily.
🚗
By Car
Take Hwy 101 north, exit at Alexander Ave for the headlands. Arrive before 9am on weekends to get parking.
🚴
Bike
Best way to experience all three areas. Rent at Fisherman's Wharf — the bridge bike path is dedicated and safe.
⛴️
Ferry from Sausalito
Golden Gate Ferry runs from the Ferry Building to Sausalito — then bike or bus to the headlands. Scenic and stress-free.
